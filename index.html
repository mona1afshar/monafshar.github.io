Competitve Swimming

I started swimming competitively at age 10 with the North York Aquatic Club (NYAC) and I continued the sport through middle and high school. Starting with just three hours of training per week, I gradually increased my dedication and commitment to the sport, eventually reaching 16 hours a week. Swimming demands immense perseverance, as it involves spending long hours staring at the black line on the bottom of the pool, but this has taught me the importance of time management. Balancing 16+ hours of training with a full high school course load and extracurricular activities wass no easy feat, but it has helped me develop valuable skills and discipline. Although, I have since retired from competing, I still find time to head to the pool for a swim to clear my mind.
